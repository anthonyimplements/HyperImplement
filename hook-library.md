# Hook Library — HyperImplement / Anthony Implements

A reusable swipe file for scripting YouTube and short-form video hooks in the AI-for-business-owners niche. Pull a pattern, fill the blanks, test 2-3 variants per video.

**Core rule:** You have ~3 seconds on short-form and ~6-10 seconds on long-form before the viewer decides to stay or scroll. The hook must promise a payoff, create a curiosity gap, or trigger an emotion — *before* you introduce yourself or the topic. The brand promise ("we implement it, we don't just talk about it") is itself a hook angle: lead with the *build*, not the theory.

---

## Long-Form Hook Patterns

### 1. Result-First Reveal
**Formula:** Show the finished outcome in the first 5 seconds, *then* promise to walk them through it.
**When to use:** Build-with-me videos, tutorials, anything with a tangible deliverable (a website, an automation, a working agent).
**Templates:**
- "This entire [website / customer-intake system] was built by AI in [X] minutes — and by the end of this video, yours will be too."
- "What you're looking at is a [booking automation] that runs my [client's salon] with zero staff. Here's exactly how I built it."

### 2. Bold Claim + Proof
**Formula:** Make a claim that sounds too big, then immediately back it with on-screen evidence so it reads as fact, not hype.
**When to use:** Topic-authority videos, tool comparisons, ROI-driven content for skeptical owners.
**Templates:**
- "I replaced a [$4,000/month] task with a [free] AI workflow — and here's the screen recording proving it."
- "A non-technical business owner can now build software. I'm going to prove it to you in the next [10] minutes."

### 3. Open Loop / Curiosity Gap
**Formula:** Tease a specific, intriguing payoff you withhold until later — the viewer stays to close the loop.
**When to use:** Longer videos (8+ min) where you need to hold attention through a build.
**Templates:**
- "There's one Claude Code command that does [80%] of the work — but most people never find it. I'll show you where it's hiding."
- "By minute [6], I'll show you the exact prompt that turned a blank page into a working [invoicing app]. First, the setup."

### 4. Negative / Contrarian
**Formula:** Attack the common advice or the obvious approach, then offer the better way.
**When to use:** Establishing a differentiated POV; cutting through AI-hype fatigue.
**Templates:**
- "Stop paying for [ChatGPT Plus] to run your business. For most owners it's the wrong tool — here's what actually works."
- "Everyone's telling small businesses to 'use AI.' That advice is useless. Here's what 'using AI' actually means when you implement it."

### 5. "You're Doing X Wrong"
**Formula:** Name a mistake the viewer is probably making right now. Self-relevance forces them to stay.
**When to use:** Audience-aware content; pain-point videos for owners already dabbling with AI.
**Templates:**
- "If you're copy-pasting into ChatGPT all day, you're doing AI the hard way — and it's costing you hours."
- "The reason your AI automations keep breaking isn't the tool. It's this one setup mistake almost every owner makes."

### 6. Transformation / Before-After
**Formula:** Contrast a painful "before" state with the "after," positioning the video as the bridge.
**When to use:** Workflow-automation and time-saving content.
**Templates:**
- "[Last month] I spent [12 hours a week] on [client onboarding]. Now AI does it while I sleep — here's the whole system."
- "From [a sticky-note mess] to a fully automated [lead pipeline] — this is the build that changed how my business runs."

### 7. Stakes / Cost-of-Inaction
**Formula:** State plainly what's at risk if they don't act. Earns the next 8 seconds.
**When to use:** Strategic/positioning videos aimed at business owners on the fence.
**Templates:**
- "In [18 months], the businesses that didn't implement AI won't be slower — they'll be gone. Here's where to start today."
- "Every week you don't automate [this task], you're handing [$X] to a competitor who already did."

### 8. The Question Hook
**Formula:** Ask the exact question burning in the viewer's head, then promise to answer it.
**When to use:** Beginner-oriented explainers; "what tool / where do I start" videos.
**Templates:**
- "What if a complete beginner — no code, no tech background — could build a real [business website] today? Let's find out."
- "Which AI tools actually matter for a [small business] and which are a waste of money? I tested them so you don't have to."

### 9. Live Demo / "Watch Me Do It Now"
**Formula:** No setup — you're already mid-build on screen. The doing *is* the hook. (Pure brand fit: "we implement.")
**When to use:** Screen-recorded build-with-me content.
**Templates:**
- "I'm starting a brand-new [client automation] from a blank screen right now. Follow along and you'll have one too."
- "No slides, no theory — I'm going to build [a working AI receptionist] in real time, and you'll see every step."

### 10. Specificity / Number Hook
**Formula:** A precise number signals concrete, finishable value (not vague inspiration).
**When to use:** List videos, tool-stack videos, "X tips" content.
**Templates:**
- "Here are the [5] AI tools I install for every [Chicago small business] I consult for — and why."
- "I found [3] automations that save the average owner [10 hours] a week. Number [2] takes ten minutes to set up."

### 11. Insider / "What Nobody Tells You"
**Formula:** Frame the content as privileged, hard-won knowledge the algorithm-of-gurus won't share.
**When to use:** Positioning yourself as the practitioner vs. the talkers.
**Templates:**
- "After implementing AI for [dozens of] businesses, here's what the AI influencers won't tell you."
- "There's a reason consultants charge [$10k] for this. I'm going to give it to you for free."

## Short-Form Hook Patterns

**Short-form rule:** The first frame (visual) + first ~7 words (text overlay) + first spoken line all hit in under 3 seconds. Assume sound is off — the on-screen text must carry the hook alone. One claim. Seven words max. No intro, ever.

### 1. Contrarian Claim
**Formula:** "Everything you believe about [X] is wrong" — challenge a belief, force them to stay for the why.
**When to use:** Myth-busting AI tips; cutting through hype.
**Templates:**
- "You don't need ChatGPT for this."
- "Most 'AI for business' advice is wrong."

### 2. Mistake Warning
**Formula:** Warn them about a costly error they're likely making. Fear + self-relevance.
**When to use:** Daily-tip series; quick fixes.
**Templates:**
- "Stop using AI like this — it's costing you hours."
- "This prompt mistake is killing your results."

### 3. List Tease
**Formula:** Promise a tight, countable payoff and start the count immediately.
**When to use:** Tool drops, quick-win compilations.
**Templates:**
- "3 AI tools every small business needs."
- "Save this: 4 automations under 10 minutes."

### 4. Result-First (Show, Don't Tell)
**Formula:** First frame *is* the finished thing. Caption names it.
**When to use:** Quick builds, before/after reveals.
**Templates:**
- "AI built this website in 60 seconds."
- "This automation books appointments while I sleep."

### 5. Direct Address / Call-Out
**Formula:** Name the exact person watching. "If you're a [X], stop scrolling."
**When to use:** Niching down to business owners.
**Templates:**
- "If you run a small business, watch this."
- "Business owners: you're wasting money on this."

### 6. Curiosity Gap
**Formula:** Name a payoff but withhold the how. The gap pulls them through.
**When to use:** Single-tip reveals.
**Templates:**
- "This one Claude command does 80% of the work."
- "Nobody's using AI for this — but they should."

### 7. Speed / Time-Bound
**Formula:** Compress a known-hard task into a shockingly short window.
**When to use:** "In X minutes" builds.
**Templates:**
- "Automate this boring task in 10 minutes."
- "Build a business website before this video ends."

### 8. The Question
**Formula:** Ask what they're already wondering. Pair with bold text overlay.
**When to use:** Beginner tips.
**Templates:**
- "Can a beginner really build with AI? Yes."
- "What AI tool should you actually start with?"

### 9. Negative Command
**Formula:** "Don't [X] until you've seen this." Pattern-breaks the scroll with a stop signal.
**When to use:** High-stakes tips, tool warnings.
**Templates:**
- "Don't pay for that AI tool yet."
- "Don't hire anyone before trying this AI."

### 10. POV / In-Progress
**Formula:** Drop them into the middle of an action. Visual + text imply a process underway.
**When to use:** Build-in-public clips.
**Templates:**
- "POV: AI just did your whole morning's work."
- "Watch AI write this in real time."

### 11. Big Number / Money
**Formula:** Lead with a dollar figure or dramatic stat. Money is an instant attention trigger.
**When to use:** ROI and cost-saving tips.
**Templates:**
- "This free AI replaced a $3,000 task."
- "I saved a client $2k/month with one automation."

### 12. "Save This" / Utility Tag
**Formula:** Tell them upfront it's worth saving — primes a save (which the algorithm rewards) and frames value.
**When to use:** Reference-style tip dumps.
**Templates:**
- "Save this before you touch any AI tool."
- "The only AI stack a small business needs — save it."

## Hook Anti-Patterns (Never Do)

- **Animated intros / logo stings before the hook.** A 3-second intro can cost 8-15% of your audience, and they don't come back. Brand *after* the hook, if at all.
- **"Hey guys, welcome back to the channel."** Slow social greetings, "make sure to like and subscribe" up front, "in today's video we're going to talk about…" — all dead air during the make-or-break window.
- **Burying the payoff.** Don't ramble through setup before stating what they'll get. Lead with the promise.
- **Vague claims with no specificity.** "AI can help your business" is invisible. Numbers, dollar figures, and named outcomes convert; abstractions don't.
- **Over-explaining the hook.** One claim, seven words on short-form. Too much talking = lost viewers.
- **Hook that doesn't match the payoff.** Clickbait that doesn't deliver tanks retention and trains the algorithm against you. Promise only what the video delivers.
- **Relying on audio alone (short-form).** ~80% watch muted. If your hook isn't legible as on-screen text, it doesn't exist.
- **Low-contrast / tiny captions.** Keep text big, high-contrast, on screen for at least 2 seconds.
- **Hedging language.** "I think," "maybe," "kind of" — drains authority. The practitioner brand needs declarative openers.
- **Generic faceless stock B-roll on frame one.** The first frame must communicate the topic when muted; a stock cityscape says nothing.

## Worked Examples

### (a) "How a complete beginner can build a website with AI"
1. *(Result-First Reveal)* "This is a real, live business website — and the person who built it had never written a line of code. Here's exactly how, step by step."
2. *(Question + Proof)* "Can a total beginner build a professional website with AI? I'm going to prove the answer is yes, starting from a blank screen right now."
3. *(Contrarian, short-form)* Overlay: "No code. No website builder. No problem." / Spoken: "You don't need Wix, Squarespace, or a developer — watch AI build this site in real time."

### (b) "Automate [boring task] with AI in 10 minutes"
1. *(Transformation)* "I used to spend [4 hours a week] on [client invoicing]. Now AI does the whole thing — and I'll set it up in front of you in 10 minutes."
2. *(Speed/Time-Bound, short-form)* Overlay: "Automate this in 10 minutes." / Spoken: "If you still do [data entry] by hand, this AI automation gives you your afternoon back."
3. *(Mistake Warning)* "You're doing [this task] manually, and it's quietly costing you hours every week. Here's the 10-minute AI fix."

### (c) "The AI tool stack every small business needs"
1. *(Number + Insider)* "Here are the [5] AI tools I install for every small business I consult for in Chicago — and the two everyone wastes money on."
2. *(List Tease, short-form)* Overlay: "The only AI stack you need." / Spoken: "Skip the 50 tools the influencers push — a real small business only needs these [4]."
3. *(Contrarian)* "Most 'AI tool stacks' online are built to sell affiliate links, not run a business. This is the stack I actually implement for clients."

### (d) "I let AI run my business for a day"
1. *(Open Loop / Stakes)* "For 24 hours, I let AI handle my [emails, bookings, and follow-ups] with zero input from me. By the end, I either saved a day of work — or lost a client. Let's see what happened."
2. *(POV, short-form)* Overlay: "AI ran my business for 24 hours." / Spoken: "I gave AI the keys to my whole operation for a day — here's what it got right and where it broke."
3. *(Bold Claim + Proof)* "I didn't touch my business for an entire day — AI ran all of it. Here's the screen recording of everything it did while I was gone."
