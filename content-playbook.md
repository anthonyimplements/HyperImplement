# AI-for-Business / Build-With-Me Content Playbook (2025–2026)
*Research-backed formats, titles, topics, CTAs, and captions for Anthony Implements / HyperImplement.*

## Winning Formats

### Long-Form (YouTube 8–20 min; repurpose into Reels/TikTok later)
| Format | When to use | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Screen-record build-along** ("Watch me build X with AI in real time") | Flagship weekly video. Claude Code installs, AI-built sites, Make/n8n workflows. | Highest trust-to-conversion format for consultants — viewers see the actual process, not theory. |
| **"I tried X so you don't have to"** | New tool drops, or comparing 3 tools. | Pre-frames you as the filter who saves them time. Strong for tool-selection authority. |
| **Before/After transformation** | Mini case study from real or simulated client work. | Proof. Closest thing to a testimonial. |
| **Listicle tool roundup** | Evergreen, SEO-friendly, refresh quarterly. | Numbers + density. Ranks in search, gets saved/shared. |
| **Full tutorial / "build this in 20 min"** | Teach one repeatable system end to end. | Compression + Blueprint promise; positions you as the implementer. |

### Short-Form (TikTok / Reels / Shorts / FB — 15–45s, vertical)
| Format | When to use | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **3-second problem→solution clip** | Daily volume driver. | Problem-first hook stops the scroll instantly. |
| **Speed-build / time-lapse** | Satisfying, shareable. | Visual payoff; novelty + compression. |
| **"Stop doing X manually"** (myth-bust / call-out) | Countering a common bad habit. | Identity hook — viewer recognizes themselves. |
| **Single-tip / one-trick** | Highest-frequency, lowest-effort. | One honest promise, fast value, easy to batch 7+/week. |
| **Clip of long-form** (best 30s of the build) | Repurpose every long-form into 3–5 shorts. | Free volume; funnels back to the full video. |
| **Day-in-the-life / behind-the-scenes** | 1–2x/week for personality + locality. | Builds personal brand; Chicago-grounded differentiation. |

**Cadence note:** Each long-form should spawn 4–6 shorts. Two long-forms/week = 8–12 of your 14 weekly shorts come "for free." Fill the rest with single-tip and problem→solution originals.

## Title Formulas
*(Keep ~55–60 chars, main keyword in first 3–5 words, use "I"/"you," numbers, one honest promise.)*

1. **The Compression** — `[Big result] in [tiny time]` → "I Built a Full Website with AI in 11 Minutes"
2. **The Blueprint** — `My [System] for [result]` → "My Exact Make.com Workflow That Replaced a VA"
3. **The Identity** — `[Statement about viewer] + [shift]` → "Still Answering Emails Manually? Watch This."
4. **The Authority** — `[Credential]: [claim]` → "AI Consultant Explains: The Tools Wasting Your Money"
5. **The Novelty** — `The NEW way to [result]` → "The New Way to Build Business Websites (No Coder Needed)"
6. **The "So You Don't Have To"** — "I Tried 12 AI Tools So Your Business Doesn't Have To"
7. **The Cost Anchor** — "The $0 AI Setup That Replaces a $3,000 Developer"
8. **The Mistake** — "5 AI Mistakes Small Business Owners Keep Making"
9. **The Real-Time Build** — "Watch Me Build an AI Automation Live for a Real Business"
10. **The Honest Take** — "n8n Is Overhyped — Here's What Actually Works for Small Biz"
11. **The Local Hook** — "How Chicago Small Businesses Are Quietly Using AI to Win"
12. **The Beginner Gateway** — "Claude Code for People Who Can't Code (Full Setup)"
13. **The Outcome List** — "8 Things AI Can Automate in Your Business This Week"
14. **The Curiosity Gap** — "Why I Stopped Building Websites the Old Way"

**Psychology cheat:** Curiosity, FOMO, Aspiration, Surprise. Titles that make viewers feel something *about themselves* beat titles that just promise info.

## Caption Guidelines

- **YouTube:** First 2–3 lines restate the promise + 1 keyword sentence. Add timestamped chapters, community link, call link, tools. 200–350 words. 3–5 hashtags.
- **TikTok:** 100–150 chars. Caption = SEO + curiosity gap. 3–5 hashtags (one broad + niche). One soft CTA.
- **Instagram Reels:** Hook in first 125 chars. Line breaks. 9–11 hashtags in the **first comment**, not caption. End with a question.
- **Facebook:** 50–150 chars, conversational, no jargon. 1–2 hashtags max. Front-load value, ask a direct question. Good place for the Chicago angle.
